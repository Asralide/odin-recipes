Jambalaya

What's the deal with Jambalaya?

Jambalaya was a favorite comfort food of mine growing up, and was a consistent staple in what my grandma would make for dinner. Just good creole/cajun cooking.

A nice, simple, one-pot rice dish involving vegetables, spices, seafood, and other meats like sausage. It's perfect for making up meals for a week, and is incredibly versatile, along with being a dish with a ton of history.

Ingredients

  • Celery, Bell Pepper, and Onion. Often known as the Holy Trinity of Louisianan cuisine.
  • Steamed Long Grain Rice, like Basmati
  • Creole Seasoning
  • Chicken Broth
  • Sausage and Chicken

Steps

  1. Steam Rice
  2. Season the chicken.
  3. Brown sausage, then transfer to a plate.
  4. Brown chicken, then transfer to the plate with the sausage.
  5. Saute Vegetables.
  6. Add steamed rice and toast until rice is opaque and toast-y smelling.
  7. Add the liquid and meats.
  8. Stir and let sit.
  9. Fluff and serve rice, garnish with scallions and sauce if desired.
